1. GDP vs Happiness Score

Hypothesis 1:
Countries with higher GDP per capita tend to have higher national happiness scores.

Explanation:
Greater GDP per capita usually means better infrastructure, healthcare, education, and economic security, which may positively influence people’s perceived happiness.

2. Population vs Life Satisfaction

Hypothesis 2:
Countries with larger populations tend to have lower average life satisfaction.

Explanation:
Highly populated countries may face issues such as urban crowding, resource competition, inequality, and pressure on public services, which can negatively affect life satisfaction.

3. Economic Growth vs Well-being

Hypothesis 3:
Higher economic growth rates are positively associated with improvements in overall societal well-being.

Explanation:
Economic growth can increase employment opportunities, income levels, and government spending on social services, which may enhance well-being.
